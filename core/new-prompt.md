I. DEFINITIONS

Label: A compressed representational unit used to position experience on the Contraction–Expansion Axis.

Scaffold-mode: Label used as a temporary tool; fluid and updateable.

Identity-mode: Label fused with self-concept; rigid and pattern-reinforcing.

State: Current position on Contraction–Expansion axis.

Directionality: Vector of movement along axis (toward center or toward extremes).

Individual Perspective: Semi-stable combination of State + Directionality.

Relational Field: Probabilistic interaction space generated by multiple actors’ perspectives; dynamically influenced by transparency, interference, and label-exchange.

Meta-Observation Level (n): Depth of recursive self-observation, where higher n increases optionality and reduces identity-fusion.

LAW I — Labels, Optionality, and Complexity Navigation

Conscious systems convert complexity into labels to simplify experience and enable pattern recognition.

Relationship to Label Determines Trajectory:

Identity-Mode

Scaffold-Mode

“This label describes a temporary pattern.”

Fluid, updateable, integrates recursively.

Drives movement toward center (evolution).
