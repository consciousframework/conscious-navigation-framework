### Our Foundation in the Five Laws

This Code of Conduct embodies the **Five Fundamental Laws of Conscious Navigation** in our community interactions:

2. **Directional Equilibrium**: We balance restraint and expansion in our communications
3. **Relational Emergence**: We understand that community manifests through relationship

5. **Adaptive Coherence**: Our community's trajectory emerges from how we relate to each other

### Community as Relational Field

We treat our community as the primary terrain where conscious navigation principles are lived and demonstrated. Every interaction is an opportunity to practice what the framework describes.

## üúÇ EXPECTED BEHAVIORS

### Witness Stance in Communication

**Practice Observational Language:**
- "I observe..." rather than "You are..."
- "This pattern suggests..." rather than "You always..."
- Focus on describing behaviors and patterns without judgment

**Maintain Compassionate Detachment:**
- Engage with curiosity rather than certainty
- Hold multiple perspectives simultaneously
- Notice when you're identified with a position

### Relational Awareness in Interactions

**See Others Clearly:**
- Recognize different actor types and directions
- Understand that everyone is navigating their own conditioning
- Look for the coherent intention behind all contributions

**Calibrate to the Collective:**
- Sense the community's coherence level
- Adjust your engagement to support collective wisdom
- Notice when you're polarizing or integrating

### Embodied Engagement

**Bring Whole Self:**
- Communicate from integrated awareness
- Notice somatic signals during discussions
- Practice self-regulation when triggered

**Maintain Authentic Presence:**
- Speak your truth with care and timing
- Listen with full attention
- Respect your own and others' boundaries

## üúÉ UNACCEPTABLE BEHAVIORS

### Control Patterns to Avoid

**Restraint-Extreme Behaviors:**
- Rigid insistence on specific interpretations
- Gatekeeping or excluding others arbitrarily
- Suppressing dissent or diverse perspectives
- Creating unnecessary bureaucracy or barriers

**Chaos-Extreme Behaviors:**
- Disruptive or attention-seeking behavior
- Boundary violations or personal attacks
- Creating drama or unnecessary conflict
- Inconsistent or unreliable engagement

### Fragmentation Indicators

**Behaviors That Harm Coherence:**
- Personal attacks or ad hominem arguments
- Dismissing others' experiences or perspectives
- Intellectual dishonesty or misrepresentation
- Creating in-groups and out-groups

**Communication That Reinforces Conditioning:**
- Prescriptive language that imposes views
- Absolute claims without observational support
- Blame and shame-based communication
- Defensive or reactive engagement

## üúÑ CONFLICT RESOLUTION PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Witness the Situation

**Individual Reflection:**
- What conditioning patterns are active in me?
- What fears are driving my reaction?
- How can I return to witness stance?

**Relational Assessment:**
- Map the conflict using framework concepts
- Identify the core issue beneath positions
- Notice any mirroring or projection occurring

### Step 2: Compassionate Communication

**Structured Dialogue:**
- Each person speaks without interruption
- Focus on observations, not judgments
- Listen for the need behind the position

**Finding the Integration Point:**
- Look for shared values or intentions
- Identify the coherent solution
- Acknowledge multiple truths can coexist

### Step 3: Coherent Resolution

**Agreed Actions:**
- Clear, specific steps forward
- Mutual understanding of lessons learned
- Repair of relational connection

**Community Learning:**
- Extract wisdom from the conflict
- Update community practices if needed
- Celebrate resolution and growth

## üúÜ REPORTING & ENFORCEMENT

### Reporting Process

**When to Report:**
- Persistent control patterns causing harm
- Unwillingness to engage in resolution
- Behaviors that fragment the community
- Safety concerns or boundary violations

**How to Report:**
- Contact framework stewards directly
- Use observational, non-blaming language
- Focus on specific behaviors and impacts
- Suggest desired resolution approach

### Enforcement Philosophy

**Educational Approach:**
- View violations as conditioning patterns
- Offer opportunities for learning and growth
- Provide support for developing new capacities
- Focus on restoration rather than punishment

**Graduated Response:**
1. Private conversation and education
2. Mediated resolution process
3. Temporary moderation or cooling-off period
4. Community discussion about membership
5. Removal as last resort for persistent harm
