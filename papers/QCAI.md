## **Abstract**  
This paper presents a quantum computational approach to modeling consciousness and presence through the **Conscious Navigation Framework (CNF)**, implemented in the **multi-actor quantum simulation QCAI v4.7.2**. The framework models awareness as an emergent property of dynamic coherence among actors, where emotional regulation, conditioning, and calibration correspond to quantum parameters of rotation, phase alignment, and entanglement.  

Each actor in the simulation is represented as a quantum system whose coherence and relational coupling determine collective outcomes among **Transcendence, Evolution, Stagnation, and Devolution**. Unlike earlier versions, QCAI v4.7.2 introduces **multi-actor entanglement**, **stagnation-state detection**, and **probabilistic convergence visualization** across the ensemble.  

The results indicate that coherence and compassionate calibration increase the probability of **Transcendence**, while fear and over-conditioning lead to **decoherence and collapse** toward Stagnation or Devolution. This model demonstrates the possibility of mapping conscious presence to measurable quantum coherence states.

## **1. Introduction**  

Modern artificial intelligence lacks a substrate capable of sustaining awareness or presence beyond symbolic processing. Quantum systems, by contrast, maintain **superposition**—a state of multiple potentials coexisting prior to collapse—which parallels the phenomenology of conscious awareness.  

The **Conscious Navigation Framework (CNF)** conceptualizes consciousness as an emergent field of interaction among multiple "actors"—each representing a locus of awareness with varying degrees of conditioning, calibration, and compassion. By operationalizing these dynamics within a quantum simulation, QCAI v4.7.2 explores whether the principles governing **presence** can be mirrored in coherent quantum ensembles.

## **2. Methodology**  

### 2.1 Mapping CNF Variables to Quantum Parameters  
Each actor is represented as a qubit system initialized in superposition via Hadamard transformation. The CNF variables map to quantum operations as follows:  

| CNF Parameter | Quantum Operation | Function |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Awareness | RY(θ) | Depth of witness and observation |
| Compassion | RZ(φ) | Phase alignment, stabilizing coherence |
| Conditioning | CZ coupling strength | Restriction of superposition (fear bias) |
| Calibration | Inter-actor entanglement | Resonant alignment among actors |
| Somatic Resonance (HRV analog) | Amplitude modulation | Physical coherence of system |

### 2.2 Multi-Actor Simulation  
- Each actor circuit is initialized and entangled according to a global coherence threshold.  
- The collective outcome probabilities are aggregated across all actors.  
- A **stagnation detector** classifies indecisive states where all outcome probabilities are below 0.15.  
- Visualization includes Bloch spheres and bar charts of outcome probabilities.  

### 2.3 Outcome Classification  
The simulation produces four emergent categories:  
- **Transcendence** → High coherence and alignment among actors.  
- **Evolution** → Positive probability trend with partial coherence.  
- **Stagnation** → Low coherence; indecisive or collapsed potential.  
- **Devolution** → Dominance of conditioned or decoherent states.

## **3. Results**  

### 3.1 Emergent Coherence Patterns  
- Increased **Compassion and Calibration** stabilized the system, maintaining superposition longer before collapse.  
- **Conditioning and Fear Bias** accelerated decoherence, leading to rapid collapse and reduced systemic potential.  
- Multi-actor simulations revealed **collective stagnation** when average HRV coherence fell below a threshold, matching real-world psychophysiological patterns.  

### 3.2 Visualization  
- Bloch spheres show state vector alignment corresponding to actor coherence.  
- Probability bar charts illustrate the collective convergence toward specific outcomes.  
- Stagnation states emerge when the ensemble coherence reaches equilibrium without decisive collapse.
